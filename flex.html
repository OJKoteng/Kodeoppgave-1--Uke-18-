Fyodor Dostoevsky (sometimes spelled Dostoyevsky) was a prominent Russian novelist and philosopher of the 19th century. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest novelists in world literature and is known for his deep psychological insight, philosophical depth, and exploration of moral and existential themes. Dostoevsky's works often delve into the complexities of the human psyche, grappling with questions of morality, spirituality, and the nature of existence. Some of his most important work includes: Crime and Punishment, The Brothers Karamazov, The Idiot and Notes from Underground.

Immanuel Kant

Immanuel Kant was a German philosopher of the 18th century and one of the most influential figures in the history of Western philosophy. He is known for his groundbreaking contributions to epistemology, metaphysics, ethics, and aesthetics. Kant's philosophy, often referred to as Kantianism, revolutionized philosophical thought and had a profound impact on a wide range of fields.
